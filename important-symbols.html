Important Symbols of the Carolina Panthers

The Panthers have many important symbols and traditions. Here are a few of the most important and recognizable ones.

  • Logo

  • Jerseys

  • Stadium

  • Sir Purr

  • Keep Pounding

Logo

The shape of the Panthers logo was designed to mimic the outline of both North Carolina and South Carolina. In 2012, the Panthers changed their logo and logotype, the first such change in team history. The changes were designed to give their logo an "aggressive, contemporary look" and a more 3D feel and several details were tweaked to create a more menacing look. The revised logo uses a darker shade of blue. The logotype has been refined to be a modern reflection of the Panthers brand and was inspired by the swift, sleek nature of a panther.

Jerseys

By October 1993, when the Panthers were announced as the 29th NFL team, they had already finalized their logo and helmet design. However, the team uniforms were still a work in progress. Eventually, they decided on white, black, and blue jerseys and white and silver pants. The exact shade of blue that was chosen was “process blue,” which was a shade described as being lighter than Duke’s and darker than North Carolina’s (both being well-known colleges in North Carolina). Since then, the team’s uniforms have remained largely the same. This is mainly in part due to team owner/founder Jerry Richardson, a self-described traditionalist, saying that no major uniform changes would be made in his lifetime.

Of the Panthers’ three jersey colors, their blue alternate uniforms are the newest, having only been introduced in 2002. As per NFL rules, the Panthers are allowed to wear these uniforms up to two times per season. Usually, the white jerseys have been paired with white pants, while the blue jerseys and silver jerseys have been paired with silver pants. However, there have been several exceptions. In a 2012 game against the Denver Broncos, the Panthers wore black jerseys with new black pants, paired with their traditional silver helmets and blue socks. The choice of blue socks was made by team captain Steve Smith, Sr., who thought the pop of blue color made the uniforms a distinct appearance compared to other teams’ all-black uniforms. This turned out to be an excellent combination, as it was deemed the “Greatest Uniform in NFL History” by fans in an NFL.com fan-voted contest in July 2013. That same month, the Panthers’ team equipment manager said that the Panthers intended to use the all-black uniforms more in the future. As part of Nike’s “Color Rush” series, the Panthers debuted an all-blue uniform in their 2015 Thanksgiving Day game against the Dallas Cowboys.

After Nike became the NFL’s jersey supplier in 2012, the team’s uniforms didn’t change significantly. However, the collar was altered by featuring the phrase “Keep Pounding,” an homage to former Panthers player and coach Sam Mills. Nike had come up with the idea, and the team supported it as a way to expose newer fans to the legacy of Mills, who died of cancer in 2005.

Bank of America Stadium

Opened in the summer of 1996, Bank of America Stadium is located in uptown Charlotte and was specifically designed for football. The 75,525-seat, privately-financed, open-air, natural grass stadium is designed as a self-contained headquarters for the Carolina Panthers and includes training facilities, practice fields and administrative offices. The stadium and adjacent practice fields are located on 33 acres of land in uptown Charlotte. The stadium site has access to 30,000 parking spaces in the uptown area with easy access to restaurants and hotel rooms in the stadium's immediate vicinity.

The stadium facade features many unique elements, such as massive arches and towers at the entries, clad in building materials that accent the Panthers' black, silver and blue team colors. The interior has been designed to provide a generous ratio of concession and restroom facilities among wide and tall concourses in addition to individual cup and program holders, wide seats and state-of-the-art sound, video and scoreboard systems.

A five-year renovation project for the stadium began during the 2014 offseason, and updated many aspects of the stadium to keep it up-to-date and to make the fan experience as best as possible. In 2014, 4 escalator bays were added, as well as HD video boards, ribbon boards, and a state-of-the-art sound system. In 2015, all 153 suites were redone, with new features including more rows of seating, fully retractable windows, radiant heat and more televisions. The antenna system was also upgraded to improve cellular service throughout the stadium. In 2016, many renovations were made that focused on improving security and Internet connectivity. Walk-through magnetometers were installed at all stadium entrances, and Wi-Fi service was enhanced with more than 1,200 access points. New signage was installed on the upper level concourse, and digital menu boards and increased points of sale were added to concession stands. The lower level concourse saw similar renovations in 2017. Also, all eight silver club lounges were redesigned and the field was rebuilt as new irrigation and drainage systems were installed.

Sir Purr

Sir Purr is the official mascot of the Carolina Panthers. He is an anthropomorphized panther wearing a Panthers football jersey, No.00. Sir Purr purr-forms at a variety of events, from private birthdays to charity events. Throughout the years, he has been consistently and frequently involved with the Carolinas community. Through the Purr’s Pals program, Sir Purr teams up with Big Brothers Big Sisters of Greater Charlotte to provide a Big and Little with a lifetime memory. During the week of each Panthers home game, a Big and Little share the un-fur-gettable experience of a purr-sonal behind the scenes tour of Bank of America Stadium. A highlight of the tour is a sir-prise visit by Sir Purr, who presents the Big and Little with an even bigger sir-prise, tickets to a Panthers home game and the paw-some thrill of delivering the game ball into the stadium. Every month, Sir Purr visits the patients at the Levine Children's Hospital at Carolinas Medical Center in Charlotte and brings smiles to their faces. The 10th floor of the hospital features a special Panthers theme that includes pictures, decorations and other items that can be enjoyed by the children and their families as they receive state-of-the-art care. Sir Purr hosts the annual Sir Purr Bowl, an annual gridiron grudge match between professional and college mascots that takes place at halftime of a Panthers home game each season as popular mascots play a full-contact football game. Sir Purr caused a stir during Week 16 of the 1996 NFL season, when the Panthers were playing the Pittsburgh Steelers. On a Panthers punt that landed in the endzone, he jumped on the ball even though it was still live. This turned the play into a touchback, and Sir Purr was subsequently recorded as having 1 punt return for 0 yards.

"Keep Pounding"

Sam Mills signed with the Panthers as a free agent in the 1994 offseason. After playing for the Panthers from 1995 to 1997, he retired. Immediately upon retiring, Mills became a defensive coaching assistant for the Panthers before being promoted to the role of linebackers coach in 1999.

In August 2003, Mills was diagnosed with intestinal cancer. Although he was told he had only a few months to live, he underwent chemotherapy and radiation and continued to coach. He was an inspirational force in the Panthers’ journey to Super Bowl XXXVIII. On a cold, overcast Friday in January 2004, then-coach John Fox gathered the team on the practice field near Bank of America Stadium. The day after, the team would begin its run to Super Bowl XXXVIII with a game against the Dallas Cowboys in the Divisional Round. Mills would deliver a powerful and inspirational speech. "When I found out I had cancer, there were two things I could do: quit or keep pounding," Mills said that day. "I'm a fighter. I kept pounding. You're fighters, too. Keep pounding!"

He continued to coach the team during the 2004 season, but after battling with cancer for almost two years, Mills died at his home in Charlotte, North Carolina on the morning of April 18, 2005. Though Mills was gone, his words lived on, and “Keep Pounding” was soon adopted as the team’s motto.

In 2012, the Panthers introduced the Keep Pounding Drum, inspired by Mills’ aforementioned speech. Before every home game, an honorary drummer hits the six-foot tall drum four times to signify the four quarters of an American football game. According to the team, the drummers "come from a variety of backgrounds and occupations, but all have overcome a great trial or adversity that has not only made them strong but also pushes them to make others around them stronger." Such drummers include 2-time NBA MVP and Charlotte native Stephen Curry of the Golden State Warriors.
